The Division 5, year 2000, dataset was copied and used as a base for the 2005 dataset. The dataset was used in combination with the 2005 NAIP aerial imagery to refine field boundaries and identify additional potentially irrigated fields based on a visual assessment.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Multidate Landsat 5 images were composited and run through the NDVI model to obtain NDVI composites, which were then used to determine irrigated vs non-irrigated lands, and also where fields needed to be divided for potentially different crops.
Ground truth fields were used to determine specific crop signatures using the NDVI composites, via the Zonal Statistics tool in ERDAS on each imagery date. These signatures were then combined in Excel and outliers were removed from the ground truth pool.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Crop Type assignment: Crop types were assigned based on the classified majority crop type in each parcel. Where satellite images overlapped, crop type was assigned based on the most accurate scene analysis. Fields initially determined to be fallow during the field boundary and NDVI review, or classified as fallow, were double checked for accuracy. Parcels noted as orchards in 1993 or 2000 were checked and reclassified as orchard where applicable, as well as those parcels notes as orchard during the field boundary review.
Process_Date: 2009
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
QA/QC The draft shapefile without crop types was sent to the Division for review. Water commissioners provided parcel boundary and WDID assignment edits, and in some cases, additional information on crop type.
This data was checked for topology errors, and final crop types were assigned from the classification work. In cases where crop type had been provided by the commissioners, that crop was treated the same as a ground truth parcel and assigned if it conflicted with the classified crop.
Irrigated parcels were checked again in a QAQC procedure to ensure they comply with CDSS standards. This included checking all attributes including: all SW_COV attributes total 100, SW_WDID attributes are not duplicated, all SW_WDID attributes exist in the structure table of HydroBase. Edits and adjustments were made accordingly. All parcels comply with CDSS standards.

Overview_Description:
Entity_and_Attribute_Overview:
This dataset contains information on crop type, irrigation type, and water source. The water source identifies the surface water diversion ID (WDID) for each diversion structure it receives water from, percent irrigation by each diversion structure, and the type and ID of well it may receive water from. The type of well signifies whether it is a decreed well (WDID) or a closed basin permitted well (Receipt Number). The WDID and Receipt number will relate to the HydroBase Structure and WellApplication tables, respectively.
